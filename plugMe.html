Overview

 

In my role as an environmental amateur, I decided to ease the lives of electric car drivers everywhere.
In order to solve the problem of finding a station, I decided to design an app that helps you find the location of the vehicle load
- right next to you.

Dive in to The problem

The big problem of ev cars is that the charging process takes a lot of time and the driver needs to find what to do at this time. Another problem is that the charging station can be everywhere - but how can we know where? There is a private station and a public station, if it is private, it can be in your garage, so how do people know about that? And if there is someone that is already charging right now, if we will come we need to wait until he finishes, and then we can use the charger - it could be a couple of hours!!

Experiencing the problem

The best way to formulate an idea is to either understand or discover an existing problem or experience it first-hand. Then, deduce from it the relevant meanings and think of ways in which it could have been solved. It is important to remember that not everyone responds in the same way to the same challenge and therefore it is imperative to establish user research as diverse and deep as possible.   I decided to rent an electric car and find out the experience of using an electric car, charging, and driving.

Target audience

 

Experienced drivers

Experienced drivers like to discover and explore new places and do not shy away from unconventional roads. They need to know where they have charging points and do not be limited by that.

Average drivers

The average driver will want to travel without worrying about where he will be able to load the vehicle. He needs to make sure he does not "get stuck" anywhere and has enough electricity to reach basic destinations - work, scattering and picking up children, shopping, friends and family

Young drivers

The young drivers are very nervous about the drive itself. The part of finding a charging point can only add to the frustration and stress of the driver. We should simplify the situation in the best way and give them a sense of security.

  The challenge

Help drivers manage a fast and efficient search of charging stations that allows the drivers to utilize their time.

Plugshare and Grivo

 

Plugshare

Plugshare has some problems, such as non-communicate UI design. When you install the app, you need to update your car details, and all of your searches will be based on this- but if you have another car? If you want to find a station for some friends? Basically, Plugshare does not allow you to rent a station in advance, pay, know what you can find around, etcâ€¦

 

Grivo

While Grivo is a high-quality app, communicate design, navigate to the station, help option, you can know what kinds of charging stations you have, traffic, and many more! What you don't have in Grivo is: The option to make reservations. The full information about what you have around. Get a massage when the charging is finished. Some general information about a new station or new regulations. A payment method that works. The app working just in Israel, we need an international App The driver needs an app that contains all the options in one place. Now we can find Facebook groups, WhatsApp groups, and applications like Wallbox That allows managing your current station.

To encourage these people to use electric cars, we should create an easy-to-use shell

User research

Questionnaires for 27 drivers. To gather as much measurable information as possible regarding habits, preferences, ways of charging, and the main use methods. In this part, I interviewed 27 people. After analyzing the questionnaires, I learned a lot about the way the product should be developed, what should be emphasized and what should be further explored in the next stage of in-depth interviews with users

 

User interview

In-depth interviews. The purpose of the In-depth interviews is to better understand the results from the quantitative research phase. At this point, I focused on understanding behavior patterns in specific situations, preferences, and complex ways of thinking.

Personas

 

Personas are descriptive figures built on the data collected from the study, in order to represent the different types of users who will use the product, application, site, or service offered in a similar way. These can be quantified, researched and relevant lessons and thoughts for further planning and design can be derived from them. The personas will help the designer understand the needs, experiences, goals, and behavior of the users.

The solution

 
EV drivers App
 

A simple interface that will help you to find and reach the relevant point of station next to you. Focused filtering of characteristics that are important to you, and navigation to the station with detailed driving directions, accompanied by accurate photos and instructions.

The user will be able to see photos, details, if the station is occupied (and for how long), and other drivers' scores which will verify the quality, rapidness of charging, prices, and description of the station and what you have next to it.

The user will be able to share stations he liked or discovered, get a higher rating, and be exposed to new and special stations.

The user can update online, on how much time he has left to be on this station

User flow

 

User flow is a schematic flow chart that a typical user passes in order to complete a task. User flow takes the user from the entry point through actions to the desired result and the final action. In my case, I chose a User flow for a new user on his way to find the ideal station. This is one of many different types of User flows
