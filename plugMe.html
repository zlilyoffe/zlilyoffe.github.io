PlugMe

 

EV driver App - helps the driver find the best charging station.

Project Overview

As an environmental amateur, I wanted to ease the lives of electric vehicle drivers everywhere.
EV adoption introduces new challenges like charging duration and navigation to the station.
In order to address these problems -
I decided to design an app that helps the driver find the best charging station.

design process

Empathize - Define - Ideate - Prototype

One of the best ways to explore an existing problem is to go through it by yourself (if possible).
It is important to remember that not everyone responds in the same way to the same challenge,
and therefore it is imperative to establish a diverse user research.
  I decided to rent an electric car and experience driving and charging.

Dive in to The problem

Long charging time - Usually the charging power is low, what results hours of charging.
Complex navigation - Station distribution is unexpected making it hard to find them.
Waiting in line - Drivers are not coordinated and might arrive to the same station in the same time.

Market Research

Market players |   PlugShare, Grivo, Energy, EV, Israel EV club

Plugshare

PlugShare has noticeable problems :
First, when you install the app, you need to update your car details, what makes all of your searches based on this.
If you have another car? or if you want to find a station for a friend?
Second, PlugShare doesn't allow you to make reservations, pay and know what you have around.

 

Grivo

Grivo is a high-quality app with communicative design, navigation to the station, help options, and many more!
What you don't have in Grivo is:

The driver needs an app that contains all options in one place.

User research

Questionnaires for 27 drivers to gather measurable data regarding habits,
preferences, ways of charging, and the main use methods.
After analyzing the questionnaires, I learnt a lot about the way the product should be developed,
what needs to be emphasized and further explored in the next stage of in-depth interviews with users.

 

User interview

 

In-depth interviews. The purpose is to understand the results from the quantitative research phase.
At this point, I focused on understanding behavior patterns.

Personas

 

Personas are descriptive figures, built on the data collected from the study.
They represent the different types of users who will use the product, application, site, or service offered in a similar way.
The personas will help the designer understand the needs, experiences, goals, and behavior of the users.
It can help provide detailed description of the customer,
and that assists in making smart decision regarding product design.

 

The solution

 
EV drivers App
 

A simple interface, that will help you find and reach the relevant station next to you.
Focused filtering of characteristics that are important to you.

The user will be able to see photos, details, wether the station is occupied (and for how long).
Moreover, it will provide other drivers' scores which will verify the quality,
rapidness of charging, tariffs, and description of the station and what you have next to it.

The user will be able to share stations he liked or discovered, get a higher rating, and to be exposed to new stations.

The user can post online, how much time he is going to spend in the station.

User flow

 

User flow is a schematic flow chart that a typical user passes in order to complete a task.
User flow takes the user from the entry point through actions, to the desired result.
In my case, I chose a user flow for a new user on his way to find the ideal station.
This is one of many different types of user flows.
